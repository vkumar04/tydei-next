**Date:** 2026-04-20
**Ran:** `bun scripts/diagnose-tiein-rebate-capital.ts`

## Classification

- **C1 (tie-in shows $0 earned rebates):** The `contractTypeEarnsRebates`
  predicate in `lib/contract-definitions.ts` ALREADY returns `true` for
  `tie_in` (only excludes `pricing_only`). The DB snapshot has one tie-in
  contract with zero terms/tiers/rebates — any Charles screenshot showing
  $312K spend + $0 earned on a tie-in is therefore driven by either
  (a) missing `ContractTerm` rows with tiers on that contract, OR
  (b) a seeding path that never ran `recomputeAccrualForContract` for the
  tie-in after terms were added.
  **Action:** Keep the predicate test as a regression lock (it would catch
  a future regression) and ensure existing tie-in contracts are recomputed.
- **C2 (three-way disagreement in applied-to-capital):** The three surfaces
  Charles cites read from completely different sources:
  1. Capital Amortization `paidToDate` = sum of `principalDue` across
     elapsed schedule rows (from `getContractCapitalSchedule`, a forecast
     schedule derived from capitalCost/interestRate/termMonths).
  2. Header sublabel "applied to capital" = `min(rebateEarned,
     cumulativeScheduledDue)` (from `components/contracts/tie-in-rebate-split.tsx`).
  3. Rebates Collected lifetime = `sumCollectedRebates` (canonical).
  They share no reducer today; drift is inevitable.
  **Action:** Introduce `sumRebateAppliedToCapital` and route every
  "applied to capital" / "Paid to Date" surface through it.
- **C3 (UX):** Capital Amortization card today shows Remaining / Paid /
  Projected End-of-Term; no explicit Rebates-Applied + Balance-Due rows.
